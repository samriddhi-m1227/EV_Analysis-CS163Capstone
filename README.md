# Electric Vehicle Adoption Disparities in California

## Overview
This project analyzes disparities in electric vehicle (EV) adoption across California communities. While the state is rapidly transitioning toward clean transportation, adoption is not evenly distributed across ZIP codes.

Our goal is to understand how socioeconomic conditions, housing characteristics, environmental burden, and EV infrastructure (charging availability) influence EV adoption rates.

---

## Problem Statement
EV adoption may be concentrated in wealthier and more advantaged communities, while areas with greater environmental and socioeconomic burdens lag behind. Additionally, access to charging infrastructure may play a critical role in enabling or limiting EV adoption.

This project explores how structural inequalities and infrastructure access shape participation in clean transportation.
